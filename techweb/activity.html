Different games for different skills

  • Dynamic Problem-Solving: In a strategy game like Civilization, you must solve problems with limited resources, adapting to a changing map and unpredictable AI opponents.
  • Trial, Error & Iteration: Games like Dark Souls or Celeste teach players to analyze failure, learn from mistakes, and iteratively refine their approach until they succeed.
  • Algorithmic Thinking: Games like Factorio or Opus Magnum require players to design and optimize complex sequences of actions (algorithms) to automate production or solve puzzles efficiently.
  • Pattern Recognition: Succeeding in competitive shooters like Counter-Strike or puzzle games like Tetris depends on rapidly identifying and predicting patterns in opponent behavior or falling blocks.
